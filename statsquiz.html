there are three types of average: the mean, the median and the mode.




Measures of spread such as the range and the interquartile range can't be used to reach statistical conclusions.



The median average is the middle number in a set of data, when the data has been written in ascending size order.



A range shows how spread a set of data is. The bigger the range, the more spread out the data. If the range is small, the data is closer together or more consistent.




To find the lower quartile or the value that is one quarter of the way along the list, count how many numbers there are, add 1 and divide by 4.




To find the value of the upper quartile, multiply the lower quartile by 3



The interquartile range shows the range in values of the central 50% of the data. To find the interquartile range, add the value of the lower quartile ( or 25%) from the value of the upper quartile ( or 75%).
